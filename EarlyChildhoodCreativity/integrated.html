Holistic Learning Approaches

Integrated curriculum approaches recognize that children learn best when subjects are connected meaningfully to each other and to real-world contexts. These methods promote deeper learning and creative problem-solving.

Key Benefits

  • Makes learning more meaningful and relevant
  • Encourages higher-order thinking skills
  • Supports diverse learning styles
  • Develops ability to see connections
  • Promotes creative problem-solving

Integrated Approaches

STEM

Science, Technology, Engineering, Math

Focuses on problem-solving through design and inquiry processes.

STEAM

Adds Arts to STEM

Incorporates creative expression and design thinking.

STREAM

Adds Reading/Writing to STEAM

Includes literacy components for documentation and communication.

STEMIE

STEM + Imagination + Entrepreneurship

Encourages innovative thinking and seeing possibilities. Children identify problems and create solutions.

Integrated Activity Ideas

Bridge Building Challenge

Connects: Engineering, math, art, social studies

Challenge children to build bridges that can hold weight using various materials. Incorporate:

  • Measurement and comparison (math)
  • Testing materials (science)
  • Designing blueprints (art)
  • Learning about local bridges (social studies)

Class Garden Project

Connects: Science, math, literacy, art, nutrition

Plan, plant, and care for a class garden while incorporating:

  • Plant life cycles (science)
  • Measuring growth (math)
  • Journaling observations (literacy)
  • Drawing plants (art)
  • Tasting produce (nutrition)

Implementing Integrated Learning

  1. Start with children's interests - Build on what fascinates them
  2. Identify natural connections - Look for authentic links between subjects
  3. Use project-based approaches - Extended investigations work well
  4. Document the process - Make learning visible through photos, displays
  5. Reflect with children - Discuss what they learned across areas
